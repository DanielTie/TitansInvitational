2026 contest details here

The contest will be held at 10:00 AM on Friday April 10th of 2026

Location: St. Theresa Catholic Secondary School in Belleville (135 Adam Street 613-968-6993)

Divisions:

Senior

  • Contestants must be high school students.
  • Questions assume knowlege of trigonometry, quadratics and the ablility to work with text files containing multiple tokens per line

Junior

  • Contestants must be in grade 9 or 10.
  • No advanced math is required beyond the basic opperations
  • Contestants must be able to work with textfiles limited to a single token per line. This token be be an integer number, a word or a couple of words (like "turn right") but there will be no need to separate the words on a line to answer any of the questions.

Contest Format:

  • Contestants must be high school students.
  • Teams of up to 4 sharing one computer
  • Students will have 3 hours to complete 5 questions
  • Each question must be solved by writing a program
  • 2 attempts may be made at each question
  • Points are awarded for accuracy, time and there is a perfect run bonus (for first attempt only)
  • All questions will be based on STEM featuring themes from varioussciences, mathematics and engineering. Past themes were 'The Solar System' and 'Security'
  • Questions will include all relevant information, contestants are not expected to know facts about the theme ahead of time, it is more the theme of the questions.
  • You must download password protected data files before the contest.
  • Practice questions are available and will be posted to the contest website.
  • Schools may enter more than one team (if space is available)

Snacks and lunch is provided for contestants and coaches

There is an entry fee of approximately $10 per person (to cover the food and trophy, catered lunch and snacks are a big part of the experience for the students)

Email Daniel Tie Ten Quee at tietenqu@alcdsb.on.ca to enter your school.
